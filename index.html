Biography

Postdoctoral Appointee

Argonne National Laboratory
Current

I am currently a Postdoctoral Appointee at Argonne National Laboratory. My research investigates how different functional groups and polymer compositions in hydrocarbon-based proton exchange membranes influence proton conductivity for fuel cell applications, with the goal of developing membranes that operate at high temperatures and low humidity while reducing reliance on PFAS-based materials.

PhD in Chemistry

Michigan State University
Alumnus

I earned my PhD in Chemistry from Michigan State University, where I studied the mechanism of ammonia oxidation, a reaction directly tied to clean energy conversion as ammonia serves as a hydrogen carrier. Through multinuclear NMR spectroscopy and targeted synthesis, I published my findings as first author in the Journal of the American Chemical Society and Inorganic Chemistry.

Chemistry Research

Bridging catalysis and membrane science
to address energy challenges

From mechanistic catalysis to membrane engineering, my research has consistently centered on the chemistry underlying clean energy systems. With 13 peer-reviewed publications and a pending patent, I bring a strong foundation in materials synthesis, advanced spectroscopic characterization, and electrochemical methods, and am actively seeking to apply this expertise in materials and energy R&D.
