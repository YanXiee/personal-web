## Research Narrative (as described on portfolio)

Pin's work connects **molecular composition and structure** to **macroscopic material performance**. At MSU, he studied why certain ruthenium complexes catalyze ammonia oxidation by tracking reaction intermediates through NMR and synthesizing model compounds. At Argonne, the same structure-property logic applies to polymer membranes: understanding how sulfonation or chain architecture affects proton conductivity.

Key strengths:
- **Structure-Property Thinking**: reads characterization data to explain why materials behave the way they do
- **Interdisciplinary Collaboration**: has worked with NMR specialists, computational chemists, synchrotron scientists (APS), and industry partners
- **Independent Problem-Solving**: identifies the right experiment to resolve an open question (settled disputed reaction mechanism, diagnosed reproducibility issues in synthesis)
